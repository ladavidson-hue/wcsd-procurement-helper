Purchase Pathfinder

Answer a few questions. Get the right process, the right forms, and a checklist.

Need to buy something? Start here.

Procurement rules change based on what you're buying, how much it costs, and whose money you're spending. This tool walks you through the decisions so you don't have to memorize AM 3325. One rule never changes: no goods are ordered and no services begin until a fully executed Purchase Order (and signed contract, where required) is in place.

Question 1

The informal solicitation process, start to finish.

When a purchase is too big to just buy but not big enough for a formal bid, you run an informal solicitation: you ask several qualified vendors for proposals, score them fairly with a rubric, and recommend the winner to Purchasing. Your school or department runs it - this page shows you how, with ready-to-use templates.

When do I need one?

Amounts are one-time, single, and/or annual aggregated totals with a vendor. When in doubt, ask Purchasing - and always confirm current thresholds before you send anything.

The process, step by step

  1. Confirm the basics. Budget exists (or FRC approval is in hand), the funding source is known, and the dollar amount actually calls for an informal solicitation - check the table above or run the Purchase Pathfinder.
  2. Write the solicitation letter. Open the letter template and define the scope of work as numbered activities - every activity names a deliverable and an audience. Set proposal requirements, page limit, and the response deadline.
  3. Set the evaluation criteria BEFORE you send. Fill in Table 1 with criteria that mirror the proposal requirements, with weights totaling 100%. Vendors must know exactly what is being scored - and the rubric weights must match what you publish.
  4. Send to at least three qualified vendors. Email the solicitation and keep a copy of the email and the vendor list. Every vendor gets identical information - that's the law and the deal.
  5. Set up the scoring rubric. Fill in the workbook's setup tab, enter the same criteria and weights, and give each of your (typically three) scorers their own tab. Anyone with a personal or financial relationship to a bidder must recuse.
  6. Score independently, then roll up. Each scorer reviews all proposals alone, scoring only what the proposal says - with a written justification for every score. The All Scores tab averages the totals; the highest average wins.
  7. Recommend the award to Purchasing. Send Purchasing your recommendation with the solicitation letter, all quotes/proposals received, and the completed rubric workbook. For $25,000+, this recommendation-of-award step is required.
  8. Contract and requisition. Complete the ICA (AP-F006A + AP-F006B) or contract review (AF 3326), then submit the PR in BusinessPlus and attach: the quote-request letter/email, all quotes received, the completed rubric, the ICA/contract, insurance, and W-9. No work begins until the PO and contract are signed.
  9. Keep the file. Retain the solicitation, all responses, and all completed rubrics in the procurement file - solicitation records are kept 7 years (NRS 332.091).

Ground rules that keep you out of trouble

✓ Do

  • Publish the criteria and weights in the letter - then score against exactly those
  • Require written justification for every score
  • Give every vendor identical information
  • Verify current thresholds, insurance minimums, and fingerprinting fees before sending
  • Save everything to the procurement file

✗ Don't

  • Credit a vendor for reputation - score only what the proposal says
  • Score if you have a personal or financial relationship with a bidder
  • Change criteria or weights after proposals arrive
  • Split a purchase to duck under a threshold
  • Let work start before the PO and ICA/contract are fully signed

P-Card: what it's for, and what it's never for

Know your funding source before you swipe - general fund, grant, and SAF each have separate cards. Purchases must stay within card limits; splitting a purchase to fit is not allowed.

✓ Acceptable

  • Instructional or work-related goods within budget and card limits
  • In person, mail, phone, fax, or secure website
  • Travel purchases (registration, airfare, hotel, rental car) - only after a signed Request for Leave (HR-F530) is on file
  • School/department cards: complete the P-Card Log (AP-F014) for every purchase

✗ Never allowed

  • Personal purchases (reimbursement + discipline)
  • Software or web-based programs
  • Any technology item that goes on the network
  • Equipment needing an asset tag (unit price $5,000+)
  • Services requiring an ICA (AP-F006)
  • Textbooks or any book orders
  • Meals during travel; premium travel services
  • Food/beverage (limited exceptions per AP-P006 or with prior written CFO approval)
  • Construction services of any kind
  • Items stocked in the District warehouse
  • Gift cards, staff/vendor appreciation items
  • Alcohol or alcohol-centered events
  • Cash advances; splitting/sharing charges
  • Anything that circumvents District contracts (check the Product Catalog)
Credit limit ≠ budget. Approve transactions in Commerce Bank weekly. Credit limit increases above $5,000 require form AP-F016. Suspected unauthorized use must be reported to the Business Office and HR within 10 calendar days.

About this tool

A mockup of a self-service procurement navigator for WCSD schools, departments, and sites.

Content is drawn from Administrative Manual 3325 (Purchasing Department Manual, rev. 3.0), the Procurement of Professional Services training (CFO / Director of Procurement & Contracts), and the Business Management & Financial Controls training module. It is a navigation aid, not policy. The controlling documents are AM 3325, BP 3321, AR 3322, AR 3323, AR 3329, NRS Chapters 332 & 338, and 2 CFR 200.

Note on thresholds: AM 3325 (2022) describes IFQs at $5,000+ for non-capital purchases; the 2023 professional-services training sets quote requirements for services at $10,000+ and federal quotes at $10,000+. This tool applies the services thresholds to services and the AM 3325 thresholds to goods. Where documents differ, Purchasing's current direction controls.
